• Tap-back reactions and swipe-right replies now traverse fwdsvc
  group chats correctly. The responder side now parses SPEC §6.6
  LINKIDENTIFY (context 0xFB), so when a relayed LXMF arrives via
  fwdsvc the row is tagged with the relay's destination. A later
  reaction or reply on that bubble routes through the relay for
  fanout instead of egressing direct to the original sender
  (which would bypass the group entirely).
• 1:1 chats are unaffected — without a LINKIDENTIFY mismatch the
  tag stays null and routing falls back to the legacy direct path.
• No wire-format changes: we just parse a packet other clients
  already send. Same outbound LXMF bytes for reactions / replies;
  only the outer destination changes when a relay is in play.
