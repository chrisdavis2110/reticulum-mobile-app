Reticulum is a native Android client for the Reticulum mesh network — an
encrypted, off-grid messaging protocol that runs over LoRa radio, Bluetooth,
or any TCP link to a Reticulum transport node. Built for hams, off-gridders,
and anyone who wants a messaging client that works without cell service, Wi-Fi,
or a central server.

This app talks to an external RNode (LoRa USB / Bluetooth radio) the same way
Meshtastic or MeshCore talk to their radios. Connect over BLE, Bluetooth
Classic, or attach directly to an internet-reachable rnsd node — any
combination at once.

WHAT YOU GET

• LXMF messaging with end-to-end encryption (X25519 + Ed25519, AES-256-CBC,
  HMAC-SHA256). Identity is generated on-device; keys never leave the phone.
• Reticulum Link protocol for reliable delivery over multi-hop mesh, with
  retry and delivery proofs.
• NomadNet browser — view text pages and submit forms on Reticulum-hosted
  Nomad nodes, plus /file/ attachment downloads.
• Nodes view with OpenStreetMap (for destinations that broadcast lat/lon)
  and a force-directed Graph view showing relay topology.
• Foreground service keeps the BLE link to your RNode alive so messages
  arrive (and notify) while the app is backgrounded.
• LXMF propagation node sync — fetch messages queued for you while offline.
• Identity export / import via passphrase-encrypted .rmid archives — back
  up your identity before reinstalling.
• QR identity cards for in-person contact exchange.

WHAT IT IS NOT

• Not a standalone radio. You need an RNode (LoRa modem) — over BLE,
  Bluetooth Classic, or USB — or a TCP-reachable rnsd host. Same hardware
  requirement as Meshtastic or MeshCore.
• No account required. Identity is generated on-device on first launch;
  there is no sign-up, no cloud backup, no analytics, and no ads.
• Not a commercial service. This is open source software (AGPL-3.0).

PRIVACY

• No analytics, no tracking, no telemetry.
• No third-party advertising or analytics SDKs.
• The only outbound network traffic is whatever transport you configure
  (the rnsd host you type in, the RNode you pair to) plus OpenStreetMap
  tile fetches when you open the Nodes map.
• Crash data is not collected.

PERMISSIONS

• Bluetooth (scan + connect): pair with and talk to your RNode radio.
  BLUETOOTH_SCAN is declared with usesPermissionFlags="neverForLocation".
• Foreground service (connected device): maintain the persistent BLE
  link to your radio while backgrounded, so incoming messages can fire
  notifications. Same use case as Meshtastic's foreground service.
• Notifications: alert you to incoming LXMF messages.
